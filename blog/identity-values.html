Situation where I have made an ethical descision:

My dog was about 8 years old and was starting to slow down and not act herself anymore. I took her to the vet and found that she had cancer all over her body. About a month after I had found this out, her condition was declining rapidly. Me and my family made the decision that the best thing for her was for her to be euthanized. So we took her to the vet for the last time where she drew her last breath peacefully.

When I weighted up the options with my family, we realised that the dog would either, live for a few more months on pain and suffering or live for another week with reduced pain and suffering. We agreed that the latter was the best option for her.

An descision influenced by culture?

I don't think culture has anything to do with the decision that was made. Respect for quality of life is seen across all cultures. In general I don't associate my culture with my identity but it is fair to say that our upbringing is a major factor in who we are shaped to be even in our later lives. My family values kindness, education and hard work to get the rewards that we desire. These are also values that I hold.

My Strenghts

My Limitations

My limitaions with my career development include starting a career path for the sake of it and feeling a pressure to begin any kind of tertiary education. I have attended university before but failed to finish my degree because I lost interest in it. In hindsight the best thing to have done would have been to work until I am sure I know what my goal is in life. I think the situation could have been avoided if I had asked for help but I don't want somebody else to dictate the path my life should take so I actively avoid such situations. This is another weakness of mine. I keep within my own head and I'm to reserved and reluctant to share my thoughts and opinions on something that I think could be controversial or might change the way people think about me.

How my Strengths Will Intersect with Learning Obstacles

Being a qualified mechanic, I am able to think in a way to solve problems. Working on cars required a lot of problem solving ability. Cars are run by computers that have inputs via sensors and outputs via acuators. In other words everything is run by a system, the fault may show its symptom in one part of the system but actually be present in another part of the system. Diagnosic skills in the automotive industry require you to look at the system as a whole. Leaping to a conclusion without looking at the bigger picture can result in a misdiagnosic This skill of looking at a system as a whole could help me overcome obstacles when programming too.
